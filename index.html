FAQ

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is a day-long innovation marathon. Participants work in teams to create awesome projects from scratch, learn new skills and have some fun along the way! All skill levels are very welcome.

Who can attend?

Any enrolled undergraduate or postgraduate student, or graduate less than a year out of university. Students will be required to show their student ID on arrival.

Under 18's?

Unfortunately, we cannot accommodate under 18's at this moment in time. However, in the future we endeavour to run an event for young people. Check the HackSoc Sussex webpage regularly for more information.

Cost?

SussHacks is a free event!
This means tickets and meals are provided free of charge, funded by our generous sponsors.

Team Sizes?

It's up to you! However, only teams of 4 or fewer are eligible to win prizes.

Overnight Stay?

Sure! We'll have a variety of options for sleeping rooms available, though we recommend you bring some things to make yourself comfortable.

What should I bring to the event?

All you need is your laptop and student ID. Although it may be worth brining things like; chargers, comfortable clothes, toiletries and a sleeping bag or blanket. We want you to feel at home.

Headstart?

Only projects started at the hackathon will be eligible for prizes.

Code of Conduct?

Yes. We follow MLH’s code of conduct, which all attendees, sponsors, volunteers, and staff are required to agree to. We expect all of our attendees to be respectful and considerate of others.
